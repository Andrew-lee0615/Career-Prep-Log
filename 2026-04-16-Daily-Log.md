## 1. Deep Dive: Maximum Drawdown (MDD)
* **Definition:** The maximum observed loss from a peak to a trough of a portfolio or asset before a new peak is attained. It represents the "worst-case scenario" for an investor during a specific period.
* **Formula:** $$MDD = \frac{\text{Trough Value} - \text{Peak Value}}{\text{Peak Value}} \times 100 (\%)$$
* **Risk Application:**
    * **Baseline:** While SQL simulations calculate the *immediate* margin impact, MDD provides a *historical baseline* for how deep a sector's stock price can collapse.
    * **Stress Test:** Benchmarking the **$264B tariff shock** against historical MDD levels (e.g., 2018 Trade War) to assess if the current downside risk is unprecedented.
* **Strategic Insight:** Analyzing whether high-exposure sectors like **Retail and Automotive** are likely to breach their historical MDD thresholds due to current policy shifts.


## 2. Macro Correlation: Currency Risk (FX Translation Risk)
* **Background:** U.S. Tariffs → Potential Inflation → High Interest Rates → **Strong USD (Dollar Strength)**.
* **Impact on Multi-national Corps (MNCs):**
    * **Translation Risk:** When companies like Apple or Microsoft convert their global earnings (Euro, Yen, Won) back into USD, a stronger dollar results in "lower reported revenue/profit" on the financial statements.
    * **Competitive Risk:** A strong dollar makes U.S. products more expensive for foreign buyers, potentially reducing global market share.
* **Key Insight:** Risk analysis must account for both **Direct Costs (Tariffs)** and **Macro Side-effects (Currency Volatility)**. 
    * *Ex:* High-tech sectors may have low tariff exposure but high **FX Translation Risk**.
